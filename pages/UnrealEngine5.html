Demo.

Processes.

At the beginning of the level, a "BP_Door" is created, which contains a door frame, the door itself, a collision box, and a hand Widget. The Interact message is obtained through the Line Trace by Channel node combined with a "BPI_Interaction interface. When the player interacts for the first time, they gain access to the key.

The first, second, and third doors are the same Blueprint. The variation between doors is achieved by creating a node in the Construction Script to set the Static Mesh, which is exposed as a public variable.

Similarly, the open and closed states are controlled using a public boolean. Different messages are handled through Line Trace by Channel, since "BP_Door" contains a reference to the player, allowing it to display a custom message from there.

When the player sees the monster for the first time, it is triggered by a collision box located on the staircase, which activates a transition and a camera shake in the Level Blueprint.

Both the flashlight and the key from the beginning of the level are children of the same "BP_Parent". Widgets associated with collectible objects use a Find Look At Rotation node, allowing the Widget to track the player.

When the player obtains the flashlight, a Widget with animated elements appears, displaying a pre-rendered image of the flashlight asset, which was created in Blender. After this, the player can project light using a Spotlight attached to a Child Actor Component inside the player Blueprint.

The wall levers can be moved whether there is electricity or not, but they become locked once the puzzle is solved. If the puzzle is already solved and the electricity is activated, the camera blend animation and the door sequence will be triggered.

When the electricity is activated for the electrical device on the wall, mannequins will appear along with their collision and head animations. When the player looks back, a camera shake will be triggered. Additionally, the TV screen material will change, and the light at the end of the basement will turn on.

When the basement key is obtained, an enemy will spawn and chase the player. The basement will be sealed by a portal, and the mannequins will have physics enabled, but gravity disabled, along with some objects placed to obstruct the player's path.

The mechanic when activating the portal requires the player to point the flashlight at it to gradually shrink and eventually destroy it. To achieve this, a "BP_Portal" was created, which contains:

  • A plane with a custom material to simulate the portal effect.
  • A collision box to detect whether the flashlight is on or off.
  • A Line Trace by Channel, ensuring the player is looking directly at the portal, making the flashlight the central element of the interaction.

Once the portal is destroyed, the player will gain access to the basement exit.

If the player escapes the NavMesh of the basement, the enemy will return to a specific point where it will be destroyed by the Level Blueprint. Additionally, the objects that had their gravity disabled will revert to being static.

At the end of the demo, when the player obtains the key and interacts with the final door, an animation will be triggered. The enemy will spawn, activating the camera blend and executing the final attack animation, leaving the player immobile and sending them r to the main menu.

Extra notes.

The demo includes Level Streaming Volumes to activate and deactivate sections of the map, improving performance.

Several parts of the map include Niagara systems to simulate dust and enhance the atmosphere of the level.

In the basement, there is a cube with a localized fog material to enhance the suspenseful atmosphere.

If the player is caught by the enemy in the basement, the final attack animation will be triggered. Depending on where the player is looking, they will turn around, and the enemy's spotlight will also rotate to simulate that the player turned with the flashlight.

If the player has the flashlight but it is turned off, it will automatically turn on.

The game includes localization for Spanish.

The game includes resolution settings, scalability options, FPS settings, and more. These settings are saved in a GameInstance and in a SaveGame.

The game demo was presented in a PC build package.
